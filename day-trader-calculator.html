Why Track Your Trading Returns?

Successful traders track every trade meticulously. Knowing your exact return percentage helps you:

  • Evaluate trading strategy performance
  • Identify profitable patterns and setups
  • Calculate realistic profit targets
  • Manage risk and position sizing effectively
  • Track progress toward financial goals
  • Maintain accurate trading journals

Understanding P&L Calculations

Gross P&L is your raw profit or loss before any charges. Net P&L is your actual profit or loss after deducting brokerage, STT, exchange charges, and other transaction costs. The Return Percentage shows your net P&L as a percentage of your capital used, giving you a clear picture of your trading performance.

Tips for Day Trading Success

  • Always factor in all charges when calculating returns
  • Set realistic profit targets based on your capital
  • Use stop-loss to limit potential losses
  • Track every trade to identify what works
  • Focus on percentage returns, not absolute amounts
  • Maintain consistent position sizing
