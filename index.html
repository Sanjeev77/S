Follow this comprehensive guide to maximize the potential of our financial planning calculator and get the most accurate results for your financial goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Get Answers to Common Questions

Find answers to the most frequently asked questions about financial planning and using our calculator.

Calculator Usage
How accurate are the calculator results?

Our calculator uses proven financial formulas and industry-standard assumptions. Results are estimates based on your inputs and should be used for planning purposes. Actual results may vary due to market conditions, tax changes, and other factors. Always consult a financial advisor for personalized advice.

Is my financial data safe and private?

Yes, absolutely! All calculations happen locally in your browser. Your financial data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to our servers. We don't store, access, or share any of your personal financial information.

Can I save my financial plan?

Yes! You can export your complete financial plan as a JSON file using the "Download JSON" option. This file can be imported later to restore your plan. You can also generate a shareable image of your financial summary.

What returns should I expect for investments?

Conservative planning suggests 8-10% for balanced portfolios and 10-12% for equity-heavy portfolios over long term (15+ years). For shorter terms (5-10 years), expect 6-8%. These are historical averages and future returns may vary.

Financial Planning
How much should I save for retirement?

A good rule of thumb is to save 25-30 times your annual retirement expenses. If you need ₹5 lakhs per year in retirement, aim for a corpus of ₹1.25-1.5 crores. Start early to benefit from compounding - even ₹5,000/month at age 25 can build a substantial retirement corpus.

What's the ideal emergency fund size?

Maintain 6-12 months of expenses as emergency fund. For salaried individuals, 6 months is sufficient. For business owners or those with variable income, aim for 12 months. Keep this in liquid instruments like savings accounts or liquid mutual funds.

Should I pay off loans first or invest?

It depends on the loan interest rate. If your loan interest rate is higher than expected investment returns (e.g., credit card debt at 18-24%), prioritize paying off debt. For home loans at 8-10%, you can invest simultaneously as long-term equity returns typically exceed these rates.

How often should I review my financial plan?

Review your financial plan every 6 months or after major life events (marriage, child birth, job change, salary increment). Annual reviews are minimum. This ensures your plan stays aligned with your changing circumstances and market conditions.

Investments & SIP
What's the difference between SIP and lump sum investment?

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) involves investing a fixed amount regularly, which averages out market volatility (rupee cost averaging). Lump sum means investing the entire amount at once. SIP is better for regular income earners and reduces timing risk, while lump sum can be better if you have surplus funds and markets are low.

How much should I invest in equity vs debt?

A common rule is "100 minus your age" in equity. For example, at age 30, invest 70% in equity and 30% in debt. However, this varies based on risk tolerance, goals, and market conditions. For long-term goals (10+ years), you can have higher equity allocation.

Which mutual funds should I choose?

Focus on low-cost index funds or well-performing active funds with consistent track records. For beginners, consider diversified equity funds, balanced advantage funds, or target date funds. Always check expense ratios (prefer <1.5%) and fund manager tenure. Consult a financial advisor for personalized recommendations.

Can I change my SIP amount later?

Yes! Most SIPs allow you to increase, decrease, or pause your investments. Many funds offer SIP top-up features where you can automatically increase your SIP amount annually (typically 5-10%). This helps you invest your salary increments systematically.

Risk & Safety
What if I can't achieve my financial goals on time?

Don't panic! You have several options: 1) Extend your timeline, 2) Increase monthly investments, 3) Reduce goal amounts, 4) Optimize your investment strategy for better returns, 5) Look for additional income sources. The key is to start early and stay consistent with your investments.

How do I protect my investments from market crashes?

Diversification is key: spread investments across asset classes, geographies, and time. Maintain asset allocation discipline, don't panic sell during market downturns, and keep investing through SIPs to benefit from rupee cost averaging. Have adequate emergency funds so you don't need to withdraw investments during emergencies.

Should I invest if I have existing loans?

Yes, but prioritize high-interest debt first. Pay minimum EMIs on low-interest loans (like home loans) and invest the surplus. For high-interest loans (credit cards, personal loans), focus on clearing them first. Once high-interest debt is cleared, allocate more towards investments while maintaining loan EMIs.
