How This Calculator Works

The Health Insurance Gap Calculator estimates potential medical expenses based on your family structure, city of residence, and health history. It helps you understand if your existing health insurance policy (or lack thereof) leaves you financially vulnerable.

Why Health Insurance Gaps Are Common

Many families in India rely on corporate insurance (typically ₹3-5 Lakhs) or old policies that haven't kept up with medical inflation. With rising healthcare costs, a single major hospitalization can easily exceed basic coverage limits, leading to significant out-of-pocket expenses.

Assumptions Used

  • Base Costs: Estimated average costs for a major hospitalization event in Tier 1 (₹5L), Tier 2 (₹3.5L), and Tier 3 (₹2.5L) cities.
  • Risk Multipliers: Costs are adjusted for risk factors like age (Senior Citizens), pre-existing chronic conditions, and recent hospitalization history.
  • Medical Inflation: General medical inflation trends are factored into the risk assessment logic.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much health insurance is enough in India?
Experts generally recommend a cover of at least ₹10-15 Lakhs for a family of four in a Tier 1 city, and ₹7-10 Lakhs for smaller cities. However, this depends heavily on age and health conditions.

Is ₹5 Lakh health insurance sufficient?
While ₹5 Lakh was considered standard a few years ago, it is often insufficient today for critical illnesses, major surgeries, or prolonged hospital stays in private hospitals.

What increases hospitalization costs the most?
Room rent limits, ICU charges, consumables costs (often not covered), and complex surgeries are the biggest contributors to high hospital bills.
